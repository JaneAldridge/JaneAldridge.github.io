There are several case studies from 2014 – 2018 concerning GDPR related issues and breaches. Chose a case study and answer the following questions:

1.    What is the specific aspect of GDPR that your case study addresses?

The case study address’s the personal data obligations of GDPR, which are covered by the GDPR articles 33, 34, 58, 83 and recitals 75, 85-88 (ico, 2022). The ico (2022) states that :

‘A personal data breach means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data. This includes breaches that are the result of both accidental and deliberate causes. It also means that a breach is more than just about losing personal data.’

British airways suffered a data breach which affected 420,000 customers, where personal data such as customer names, address’s, payment card information was compromised by a cyber attack.

 

2.    How was it resolved?

British airways was fined 20 million pounds and also reached an out of court settlement with the victims of the data breach, which was ‘confidential’ (Simpson Millar, 2022)

 

  1. If this was your organisation what steps would you take as an Information Security Manager to mitigate the issue?

The data breach was caused by a Javascript used on the website, which was exploited in the cyber attack.  The Javascript ‘Modernizr’ was a third party piece of software.  The issue was that BA had not patched Modernizr since 2012, although problems were known to exist.

GDPR states that technical and organisational measures need to be taken to protect the integrity and confidentiality of personal data.

As a security manager, I would enforce strict patching of all software on a monthly basis, and there would be no exceptions. I would also put in place a process for ‘emergency’ patching, where a vulnerability has been found which could cause a data breach.  In addition I would have a process to monitor vulnerabilities in third party software, for example OWASP maintains a list of vulnerabilities. I would also hire an external party to carry out penetration testing, put in place regular risk assessments, and ensure that internal and external security audits took place on a six monthly basis.
